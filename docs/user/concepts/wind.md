# Wind

Wind is one of the most important forces in Windfall. It determines which directions are fast, which are slow, and which are blocked entirely.

## Wind Direction

At any moment, wind blows in one of six directions across the entire map. Sailors name wind by where it comes **from**, not where it goes. A northerly wind blows from the north toward the south. The direction shifts gradually over the course of the game — usually staying steady, occasionally veering or backing by one or two steps, rarely reversing entirely. A wind face in the right panel shows the current wind direction; its plumes point in the direction the wind is blowing toward.

## Heading

Your ship always faces one of the same six directions. Heading is set by the direction of your last move. At the start of a new game, your ship faces directly downwind — the most favorable starting position.

## Points of Sail

**Point of sail** is the angle between your ship's heading and the wind's origin. Because both wind and heading use the six hex directions, there are exactly four possible relationships:

| Point of Sail | Steps from windward | Moves available |
|---|---|---|
| In irons | 0 — heading directly into wind | 0 in windward direction; other directions work normally |
| Close reach | 1 — heading mostly into wind | 1 |
| Broad reach | 2 — heading mostly with wind | 2 |
| Running | 3 — heading directly with wind | 3 |

The game gives your ship a movement budget each turn. Each hex you travel costs more or fewer moves depending on the angle to the wind. Running is the cheapest direction (3 hexes available); close reach is the most expensive (1 hex); the windward hex is always blocked entirely.

**In irons** means your bow points directly into the wind. The windward hex — the one directly ahead — is blocked: you cannot sail into the wind. But you can still turn and move in any other direction. Getting caught in irons does not strand you; it means your next move must be a turn rather than a straight run upwind.

**Close reach** is slow sailing. The wind pushes across your bow and you can manage only one hex of movement per turn.

**Broad reach** is comfortable and fast. The wind comes from behind and to the side, filling the sails well. You can cover two hexes per turn.

**Running** (wind dead astern) gives you the most movement in this game — three hexes per turn. With the wind directly behind you, the sails fill and push you forward.

## Strategic Implications

Wind shapes every naval decision. A favorable wind direction makes certain routes fast and others slow. An enemy approaching from downwind is moving quickly; one approaching from upwind is sluggish and easy to outrun.

Wind shifts gradually as the game progresses. A route that is fast with a favorable wind becomes costly when the wind backs against you. Patient sailors plan their positions with the next wind shift in mind.

## Crew on Land

Crew are not affected by wind. They always receive 2 action points per turn regardless of conditions.
