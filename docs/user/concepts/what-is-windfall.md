# What Is Windfall?

Windfall is a turn-based strategy game of nautical exploration and conquest. You command a ship and crew on a procedurally generated world of ocean and land. Your goal is to explore the unknown, build fortified settlements, and capture your opponent's flag before they capture yours.

## The World

Every game of Windfall takes place on a unique map generated when you start a new game. The world is a hex grid of ocean and land, with coastlines, forests, grassland, and rocky terrain shaped by natural processes. You begin with no knowledge of the world — it is entirely hidden until your units discover it.

## How You Win

Each player has a flag. Your flag begins with one of your crew. At some point, you will choose a hiding place for your flag — somewhere on land, perhaps deep inside a fortification. Your opponent is doing the same.

To win, you must find your opponent's hidden flag, capture it with one of your crew, and carry it back to one of your own fortifications. If the crew member carrying the flag is destroyed, you lose immediately.

## The Shape of a Game

A full game of Windfall unfolds over many turns and may span multiple sessions. The early game is exploration: sailing coastlines, reading terrain, looking for the right place to settle. The middle game is construction: building fortifications that take advantage of natural shapes in the landscape. The late game is conflict: your expanding settlements and those of your opponent draw closer, and the race to find and capture the flag reaches its climax.
