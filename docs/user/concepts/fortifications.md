# Forts

A fort is a defensive structure your crew builds on land. It is not placed as a single object — it grows wall segment by wall segment and comes to life the moment your walls form a closed loop.

## Building Walls

Any crew member can spend a turn improving a land hex into a wall segment. Building one wall segment takes **3 turns**. Each turn of work advances the segment through visible construction stages — foundation, partial crenellations, completed wall.

Wall segments can be built on three terrain types:

- **Grassland** — the most common building material; standard defensive strength
- **Forest** — forested hexes can become walls, but then cannot become logging camps
- **Stone** — the strongest wall material; stone wall segments grant the fort an enhanced defensive rating

One improvement per hex. A grassland hex that becomes a wall cannot also become a farm. Choose carefully.

## Mountains as Natural Walls

Your crew never needs to build on mountain hexes. Mountains act as natural wall segments automatically. A chain of wall segments that passes through mountain terrain is valid — mountains close the gap without any construction cost.

## When a Fort Goes Live

A fort goes live the moment your wall segments — combined with any mountain hexes — form a **closed loop connected to itself**. The hexes enclosed by that loop become the fort interior.

You do not need to declare the fort complete. The game detects the closed loop automatically after each wall segment is built.

This means you can build forts of any shape. A small tight ring is quick to build but encloses less space. A large ring takes more turns but may enclose better terrain. A ring that uses mountain faces as walls is cheaper to close. Reading the landscape and finding the natural shapes is the skill.

## Coastal Forts

A fort that contains wall segments on land hexes adjacent to coast hexes is a **coastal fort**. Coastal forts can produce ships, which appear in the water next to the fort when ready.

Your own crew can board and leave ships at your coastal fort's shore — gates and ship-handling facilities are assumed. Enemy crew cannot disembark at your shore; the fort fires on them.

## What a Wall Does Immediately

You do not need to complete an enclosure to get value from a wall. **Any wall segment fires cannons automatically** at adjacent enemies at the end of your turn — including a single isolated wall hex. A lone stone wall on a clifftop fires the moment it is built.

This means you can establish a defensive position piece by piece, gaining tactical value with every wall segment you complete.

## What an Enclosed Fort Adds

Enclosure unlocks the full capability set:

**Crew production:** If a farm is within 3 hexes, the fort generates new crew over time.

**Ship production:** If a logging camp is within 3 hexes and the fort has a wall hex adjacent to a coast hex, it generates new ships. Ships appear on an adjacent coast hex.

**Flag operations:** You can carry the captured enemy flag into an enclosed fort to win the game. You can also hide your own flag inside one for protection.

## Defensive Strength

Stone wall segments increase the fort's resistance to cannon fire. When choosing a building site, look for stone terrain — a ring of stone walls is significantly harder to destroy than one built on grassland alone.
