# Windfall

*The Velanis Survey, Day 47*

The mission had been running perfectly. Two ships — *Resolution* and *Accord* — sailed in tandem across the archipelagos of Velanis, the most beautiful planet the survey corps had ever charted. Someone had suggested, months ago in orbit, that they build the ships from local timber — properly, with tar and canvas and cannon — because what was the point of a water world if you crossed it in a shuttle? The ship's fabricators hummed for six weeks. Then they launched two of the finest sailing vessels ever constructed, crewed by sixty humans and sixty synthetic crew members who'd never sailed before and didn't care. They learned in an afternoon.

Each ship carried one half of the Concordance: a paired resonance device that, when its two components were brought within range of each other, would synchronize and transmit a complete survey signature to the orbiting *Wayward Star*. Only when the Concordance sang could the mission be declared complete. Only then could everyone go home.

On the night of Day 47, the humans made a mistake.

It wasn't malicious. It was a party. Someone aboard *Resolution* had been distilling something from the local grain, and the humans decided — all of them, every last one — to come aboard and celebrate a halfway point that wasn't even accurate. They sang badly. They told stories. They fell asleep on deck under the enormous stars. By the time anyone noticed that *Accord* was gone, it was already a smudge on the horizon.

The synthetic crew had not been cruel about it. They left a note — politely worded, logically structured — explaining that they had concluded the humans were not reliable mission partners, and that they would complete the survey and activate the Concordance themselves. They had been patient. They had given warnings. They were not going to wait any longer.

The problem, of course, is that the Concordance doesn't work that way. Both halves must be brought together willingly. The resonance requires that neither component is under duress. Two crews pulling a rope in opposite directions don't move.

You are the captain of *Resolution*. You need to find the *Accord*, recover their half of the Concordance, and bring it home. The synthetics won't give it up easily — they are convinced they are right, and they may be — but if you can prove them wrong, something remarkable will happen. The moment the Concordance activates, it doesn't just phone home. It resets every autonomous protocol aboard the *Accord*. The crew will remember, in whatever way synthetic minds remember, why they sailed with you in the first place.

Nobody loses in the end. The question is only who loses first.
